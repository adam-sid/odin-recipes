Chile en Nogada

A Stunning Mexican Dish with a Symphony of Flavors

Ingredients

  • 6 poblano peppers
  • 1 pound ground beef or pork
  • 1/2 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/2 cup chopped almonds
  • 1/2 cup diced apple
  • 1/2 cup diced pear
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup queso fresco or feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup pomegranate seeds (for garnish)
  • Walnut sauce (recipe coming soon!)

Recipe

  1. Prepare the Poblano Peppers

    • Roast the poblano peppers over an open flame or under a broiler until the skin is charred and blistered. Place them in a plastic bag for a few minutes, then peel off the skin, make a slit, and remove the seeds.

  2. Prepare the Filling

    • In a skillet, cook the ground beef or pork over medium heat. Add onions and garlic and cook until translucent. Stir in the raisins, almonds, apple, pear, cinnamon, cloves, salt, and pepper. Cook for about 10 minutes, then let it cool.

  3. Stuff the Peppers

    • Carefully stuff each poblano pepper with the meat and fruit mixture, then close the opening with toothpicks.

  4. Make the Walnut Sauce

    • Blend the walnuts, heavy cream, cinnamon, sugar, and salt until smooth. Pour the sauce over the stuffed peppers.

  5. Garnish and Serve

    • Sprinkle the crumbled queso fresco and pomegranate seeds over the top of the stuffed peppers. Serve the Chile en Nogada with a side of white rice or warm tortillas.

Enjoy your homemade Chile en Nogada!
