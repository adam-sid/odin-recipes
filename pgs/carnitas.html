Pork carnitas

Deliciously Tender Pork Carnitas: A Flavorful Mexican Fiesta on Your Plate!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds boneless pork shoulder (or pork butt), cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 oranges, juiced (about 1 cup)
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Recipe

  1. Season the Pork

    • In a large bowl, combine the pork cubes, chopped onion, minced garlic, cumin, oregano, chili powder, salt, and black pepper. Mix well to evenly coat the pork with the seasonings.

  2. Marinate the Pork

    • Add the bay leaves, freshly squeezed orange juice, lime juice, and chicken broth to the seasoned pork mixture. Mix to combine. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, but overnight marinating is even better.

  3. Cook the Pork

    • Heat a large, heavy-bottomed skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the vegetable oil. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated pork and sear it until it's browned on all sides.

  4. Simmer the Pork

    • Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet or Dutch oven, and let the pork simmer for about 2-3 hours or until it becomes tender. You can also transfer the mixture to an oven-safe dish and cook it in the oven at 325°F (163°C) for a similar amount of time. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  5. Shred and Serve

    • Once the pork is tender and can be easily shredded with a fork, remove it from the heat. Shred the pork into smaller pieces. You can crisp it up by placing it under a broiler for a few minutes if you like. Serve the pork carnitas in warm corn tortillas with your choice of salsa, chopped cilantro, diced onions, and lime wedges.

Enjoy your homemade pork carnitas!
